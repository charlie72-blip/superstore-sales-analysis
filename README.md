Superstore Sales Analysis Dashboard

Background
Superstore is a fictional US-based retail company selling products across three categories: 
Furniture, Office Supplies, and Technology. This project analyzes 4 years of transaction data 
(2014–2017) covering 9,994 data across 4 regions in the United States.

The goal of this project is to go beyond surface-level sales numbers and answer a more important question:
Which products are actually making us money and which ones are silently draining profit?


Tools that i Used 
Python (Pandas) = Data extraction, aggregation, and metric calculation
Microsoft Excel = Dashboard design and visualization


What I Analyzed
- Overall business performance : total revenue, profit, and margin
- Sales vs Profit by Category : to identify which category is truly profitable
- Discount impact : how discounting behavior affects profit margin
- Sub-category deep dive : finding which specific products are losing money


Key Insights

 1. Furniture is a Silent Profit Killer
Furniture has the second highest revenue ($742K), but its profit margin is only 2.5% 
far below Office Supplies (17.0%) and Technology (17.4%).

 2. Heavy Discounting = Negative Profit
Two sub-categories are operating at a loss:
- Tables -> avg discount 26.1%, profit margin -8.6
- Bookcases -> avg discount 21.1%, profit margin -3.0

The more discounts given, the more money the company loses on these products.

 3. Technology is the Star Performer
Despite having the highest revenue ($836K), Technology maintains a healthy 17.4% margin 
with the lowest average discount (13.2%) proving that less discounting leads to better profitability.

 4. Office Supplies: High Volume, Healthy Margin
With 22,906 units sold (the most of any category) and a 17.0% margin, 
Office Supplies is the most consistent performer.

Business Recommendation
-The company should review its discounting strategy for Furniture, especially Tables and Bookcases. 
-Reducing discounts on these products or repositioning their pricing could significantly 
-improve overall profitability without needing to increase sales volume.


What I Learned
This project taught me that revenue alone is a misleading metric. 
A product can generate millions in sales while simultaneously destroying profit. 
The real story is always in the margin  and that's what data analysis is for.
